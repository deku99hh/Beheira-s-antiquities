بُنيت البوابة كجزء من سور رشيد الذي أمر بتشييده السلطان المملوكي قنصوة الغوري في أواخر القرن الخامس عشر لحماية المدينة من الغزوات البحرية. كانت تُعد البوابة الشمالية للمدينة، وتطل على طريق استراتيجي كان يعبر منه القادمين إلى رشيد. ورغم صمودها التاريخي إلا أنها تعاني حاليًا من الإهمال وتحتاج إلى ترميم عاجل للحفاظ عليها.

Abu Rish Gate

Abu Rish Gate is one of the most prominent historical landmarks in the city of Rosetta (Rashid). It represented the western entrance and the formidable fortress of the city during the Mamluk era. The gate gained great fame for its pivotal role in repelling the English forces during the Fraser Campaign, making it a symbol of national resistance in Rosetta.

History and Defensive Importance

The gate was built as part of the Rosetta Wall, which the Mamluk Sultan Qansuh al-Ghuri ordered to be constructed in the late 15th century to protect the city from naval invasions. It was considered the **Northern Gate** of the city, overlooking a strategic road used by those arriving in Rosetta. Despite its historical resilience, it currently suffers from neglect and requires urgent restoration to preserve it.

Surrounding Landmarks

A group of shrines is located near the gate, most notably the Shrine of Sheikh Abu Rish, which dates back to the period before the wall itself was built, reflecting the deep spiritual dimension of the area. It is also surrounded by remnants of the old Rosetta Wall, which formed the city's first line of defense during periods of unrest and military conflicts.
